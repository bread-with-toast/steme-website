LEDs & Breadboards

  • LED stands for Light Emitting Diode.
  • LEDs are a type of diode, which means they only allow current to travel in one way
  • LEDs have two leads/legs/pins. The longer one is the anode (goes to the + side of a battery) and another is the cathode (- side).
  • Breadboards are used to prototype circuits.
  • They might have two rows at the top and bottom, one for VCC (voltage) and the other for ground (-).
  • The columns in a breadboard are connected.
