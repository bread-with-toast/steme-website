Ohm's Law

  • Ohm's Law is a set of formulae which we can use to calculate current (I), resistance (R) and voltage (V).
  • Current is the speed at which electricity travels.
  • Voltage is the difference of electric charge between two points.
  • Here are the formulae for ohm's law:
  • V = I*R
  • I = V/R
  • R = V/I

    • Examples

    V = 5 volts

    I = 0.01 amps

    Resistance = 5 / 0.01 = 500 ohms

    R = 220 ohms

    I = 0.02 amps

    Voltage = 0.02 * 220 = 4.4 volts
